6.1 House-Level Hedonic Regressions

6.1.1 Setting

We employ hedonic regression analysis to quantify the monetary impact of sewage spills on residential property values. This approach statistically isolates the effect of varying sewage spill volumes within a defined proximity to homes, while controlling for other property and locational characteristics. The hedonic methodology enables us to estimate the implicit price adjustment—either premium or discount—that buyers apply when sewage spills are present.

The results below examine hedonic regressions across three spatial radii (250m, 500m, and 1000m) around each property. For each transaction, I calculate the total volume of sewage spills within the specified radius for every calendar quarter. Properties located beyond the radius boundary are assigned a spill statistic of zero. I also calculate the inverse of the minimum distance to the nearest sewage spill site, which serves as a spatial control variable, allowing us to account for the proximity effect of sewage spills on property values.

The spill exposure variables are constructed using two temporal aggregation methods:

  1. Lagged statistic: Total sewage spill volume in the quarter immediately preceding the property transaction
  2. Rolling statistic: Cumulative sewage spill volume over the four quarters preceding the property transaction

Regressions are estimated both without controls (NC) and with controls (C):

  • Old/New: A binary variable indicating whether the property was a newly built property that had not been previosly sold.
  • Property Type: A categorical variable indicating the type of property (detached, semi-detached, terraced, flats/maisonettes, other)
  • Duration: A binary variable indicating the tenure of the property (freehold, leasehold)
